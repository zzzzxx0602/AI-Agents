On the brighter side, the debt-to-equity ratio is improving, currently at 0.46, indicating a strengthening financial structure with decreasing reliance on external debts. Furthermore, the Altman Z-Score is in the safe zone (3.29), suggesting a low risk of bankruptcy.

**4. Valuation Assessment:**

However, intrinsic valuation using DCF suggests a significant overvaluation. The DCF equity value translates to a fair value per share of $129.68, significantly lower than the current market price of $239.30, implying a downside of -45.8%.

**5. Investment Risks:**

Investment risks include deteriorating ROE and asset turnover, suggesting declining efficiency. The high P/E ratio compared to peers implies potential overvaluation, which could result in depreciating share value. Additionally, the general risks associated with the sector include increased regulatory scrutiny, intensifying competition, and vulnerability to technological disruptions.
